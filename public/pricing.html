Common pricing questions
Why are agents unlimited on the free tier?
The 5-agent limit was the wrong gate. Multi-agent pipelines often have 8-15 distinct agents, and capping agents at the entry point punishes the most valuable use case before the developer has seen any value. The correct axes are commit volume and retention - not agent count. Agents are unlimited on every plan.
What exactly is a "context stored"?
A context is one stored commit record - one execution step in your audit chain. The contexts limit is the maximum number of commit records your account holds at any time. When records age past your retention window (30 days on Free, 90 days on Pro), they are pruned automatically, freeing up context slots. Read operations like replay, verify, and export never count against this limit.
What counts as a commit?
Every call to POST /api/commit counts as one commit, regardless of payload size. Read operations - replay, verify, pull, export, fork - do not count. You only pay for what you record, not how often you inspect it.
What are "hooks and policies"?
Policies are rules that run on incoming commits before they are stored. Examples: reject commits where payload exceeds a size threshold, require certain metadata fields, flag commits where model output matches a pattern. Think of them like branch protection rules in Git, but for agent commit behavior. Full documentation at Hooks and Policies.
What does "Execution audit reports" include?
DarkMatter-generated reports on execution history, chain integrity, and verification status - useful for internal review and developer audit workflows. These are distinct from formal compliance certifications (SOC 2, HIPAA, etc.), which involve third-party audits and legal agreements. If you need formal compliance documentation, contact us for Enterprise to discuss what's available and realistic timelines.
What does self-hosting give me vs the cloud product?
Self-hosting gives full infrastructure control: data stays in your environment, no usage limits, MIT license. It does not provide DarkMatter's independent audit guarantee - because you control the infrastructure, the record is no longer stored outside your system. For true third-party independence, the cloud product with BYOK is the correct path. Both are legitimate choices with different trust models.
What happens when I hit a limit?
You receive an email at 80% of your monthly commit limit. Commits over the limit return a 429 response. We do not silently drop data. Upgrade anytime to restore access immediately.
