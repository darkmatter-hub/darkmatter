FAQ
Do I need to trust DarkMatter to verify a record?
No. Verification works independently using exported proof bundles. The hash chain, signatures, and timestamp proof are all self-contained. You can verify any record offline without ever contacting DarkMatter.
Are features limited by plan?
No. Every plan includes the full Black Box: commit, replay, verify, export, share links, and API access. Plans differ only by commits per month and retention period.
What happens when I hit my monthly commit limit?
The API returns a 429 with a clear message and an upgrade link. Records already committed are never affected — the limit only applies to new commits in the current billing month.
What happens if I stop using DarkMatter?
Export your records before canceling. Every exported proof bundle is self-contained and verifiable offline forever — no account or subscription required to verify.
Is this a logging tool?
No. DarkMatter is a system of record for AI decisions with cryptographic verification. Logs can be edited. DarkMatter records cannot — tampering is mathematically detectable.
Should the proof bundle be encrypted before sharing with an auditor?
For L1 and L2 records: no. The auditor verifies the hash chain against the payload — encrypting the payload breaks their ability to verify. Share under NDA, not encryption. L3 (BYOK, roadmap) handles cases where payload confidentiality is required alongside verification.
