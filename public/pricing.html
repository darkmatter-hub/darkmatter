Common questions
What counts as a commit?
Every call to POST /api/commit counts as one commit, regardless of payload size. Forks, pull requests, and read operations (replay, verify, export) do not count.
What happens when I hit my commit limit?
We'll notify you at 80% usage. Commits over the limit are queued and will return a 429 response. We won't silently drop data. Upgrade anytime to restore access immediately.
Can I self-host DarkMatter?
Yes. DarkMatter is fully open source under the MIT license. Deploy it inside your own infrastructure with Node.js and Postgres. The self-hosted version has all features including BYOK and DID support.
What is BYOK encryption?
Bring Your Own Key means your context payloads are encrypted with a key you control before being stored. DarkMatter stores only the ciphertext and never has access to your plaintext. Only you can decrypt your data.
Does DarkMatter help with EU AI Act compliance?
DarkMatter provides tamper-evident audit trails that support regulations such as EU AI Act Art. 12 and 19 logging requirements. As an independent external layer, it addresses the credibility concern of self-reported logs. We recommend consulting your legal team on specific compliance requirements.
How do I cancel?
Cancel anytime from your dashboard. No lock-in, no cancellation fees. Your data is retained for 30 days after cancellation so you can export it.
