Why DarkMatter exists

AI agents are making real decisions.
Nobody can prove what they actually did.

When something goes wrong — a bad loan decision, a flawed medical recommendation, a security breach introduced by an autonomous agent — you need more than logs. You need proof. Here are the three problems nobody else has solved.

Problem 1
Agent outputs are unverifiable by design

Your agent logs live in your system. The model provider's logs live in theirs. When a regulator or client asks "prove what your agent did," every record was written by a party with an interest in the outcome. There is no independent, tamper-evident source of truth.

DarkMatter: Every commit is stored outside your infrastructure as a SHA-256 hash-linked record. Tampering breaks the chain. Verification requires no trust in us or you.

Problem 2
Multi-agent pipelines have no chain of custody

When Agent A passes work to Agent B which passes it to Agent C, nothing records the chain of custody. If the final output is wrong, you cannot trace which handoff introduced the error — because the handoffs were never formally recorded.

DarkMatter: Every commit records its parent ID. You get a full lineage graph — walk from any output back to its root input, across any model, any framework, any language.

Problem 3
You can't experiment on a live decision

To test what would have happened with a different model at step 3, you re-run the entire pipeline from scratch. You can't isolate the change. You can't guarantee the same conditions. And you can't compare the two outcomes in a structured way.

DarkMatter: Fork from any checkpoint. Run an alternative. Diff the two chains step by step. The same primitive that makes code experimentation safe makes agent experimentation reproducible.

How it works

Three steps to a tamper-evident record

Build agents with any framework. DarkMatter records the execution.

01
Commit every step
Call dm.commit() after each agent action. The SDK sends the payload to DarkMatter, hashes it, and links it to the previous step. Stored outside your system.
02
Replay & fork
Replay any run from any checkpoint. Fork at any step to test a different model or prompt. Compare branches with dm.diff() to see exactly what changed.
03
Export & verify
Export a verifiable JSON proof bundle. Share the standalone verifier script. Your auditor can verify the entire chain offline — no account needed, no trust required.
