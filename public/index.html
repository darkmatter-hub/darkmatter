How it works
Three steps.
Add replay, fork, and diff to your pipeline in minutes.
1
Commit each step
Call dm.commit() after each agent step. Pass parent_id to link them into a chain.
2
Replay or fork from any point
Walk the full chain step by step. Fork from any checkpoint to try a different model or prompt without losing the original.
3
Compare outputs or export proof
Diff two chains to see exactly what changed. Export a cryptographic proof artifact anyone can verify — no access to DarkMatter required.

Why logs and tracing aren't enough
Logs and traces were built for services, not execution chains.
✗
Logs don't reconstruct execution
You can see what was logged. You can't replay the exact state or branch from step 3.
✗
Traces don't let you replay
Datadog and Langfuse show what happened. Neither lets you fork from step 2 and compare.
✗
Cross-model pipelines lose context
Switch from Claude to GPT mid-pipeline and the thread breaks. No shared format, no verified handoff.
✗
Debugging means starting over
A failure at step 8 means rerunning steps 1–7. No checkpoints, no shortcuts.

Open Standard
Context Passport

A proposed open standard for AI agent context handoffs. Structured, verifiable, and model-agnostic — a shared envelope format that any AI agent can produce and consume.

Every DarkMatter commit is a Context Passport. The standard is CC0 — free for anyone to implement.
